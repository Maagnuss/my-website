+ Study contents

Mission Future: digital design and production

The buildings, neighbourhoods and cities of the future are smart, connected, climate-neutral and think for themselves. As a digital designer and production engineer, you are at the centre of this change and part of the digital transformation in construction. You can help shape the future of the intelligently networked city. With the multidisciplinary skills you have acquired and your up-to- date knowledge of IT, construction technology and architecture, you are ideally equipped to develop and apply creative solutions for the city of the future. Thanks to you and the digital tools, the challenges of the future such as urbanisation, sustainability and increased efficiency will be mastered in a more transparent, effective and targeted manner.


The main contents of the degree programme can be summarised in these subject areas:

  • Mediation of the digital process chain in the construction industry (design, planning, realisation and conversion of construction projects)
  • Industrial planning and production processes
  • Communicating social relevance and an understanding of sustainability and resources
  • Partnership-based construction

The degree programme is further characterised in a special way by these aspects:

  • Digitalisation, AI and Working World 4.0 as a guideline for all modules
  • Strong foundations and intensive in-depth programmes
  • Occupational field-specific competences
  • Strong internationality
  • Students have a wide range of options
  • Good foundation for a future Master's degree programme




+ Study programme

The degree programme lasts seven semesters and graduates receive a Bachelor of Engineering degree. The course is divided into the construction and digital fundamentals phase (semesters 1 to 4), the practical semester (semester 5) and the integral project phase (semesters 6 to 7). An elective module from the field of general sciences is also integrated. In order to promote the integral character of vocational training, the three main subject categories run through the entire course of study. A larger proportion of construction-related content is taught at the beginning of the programme, as digital implementation builds on an understanding of the fundamentals of construction.





+ Dual study

A dual study model combines the advantages of an academic degree programme with the benefits of vocational training or in-depth practical phases in companies. The dual study programmes combine theory and practice in high-quality dual study programmes in such a way that students and companies benefit equally.

Detailed information on the different options, the process, the application and the cooperation partners can be found on the general page on the dual study programme at Augsburg University of Applied Sciences.

If you as a company are interested in a co-operation within the framework of the dual study programme, you can find out more on the university's dual study programme page. Further information on dual study programmes from a company perspective can be found on the hochschule dual website.

+ Career prospects

The ‘digital design and production’ Bachelor's degree programme aims to prepare students for the entire spectrum of digitalisation in the construction sector. Due to different challenges, greater complexity and the increasing demand for digitalisation, not only the need but also the requirements for graduates of construction courses are increasing. The degree programme takes particular account of this need and the changed requirements profile.





+ Application and admission

The degree programme will be offered as a full-time course from the winter semester 2022/23; the target groups of the Bachelor's degree programme are:

  • Persons authorised to study at a university
  • Graduates of technical colleges and technical schools with technical training specialisations and corresponding entry requirements.
  • Prospective students with work experience and a university entrance qualification who want to develop their skills and continue their education.
